TUR14 — Academic Staffing by Employment Type: Headcount, FTE and Costs

HESA Staff Tables 1, 7 and 11, plus UEL's own Annual Report staff notes, 2014/15–2024/25. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

TRADE UNION REPORT SERIES · TUR14
HEADLINE UEL has no single published table that gives headcount, full-time-equivalent (FTE) and cost together, cut consistently by full-time/part-time, fixed-term/open-ended, and guest/hourly-paid lecturer (HPL) status. This report brings together three genuinely different, independently-sourced counting systems – HESA's Staff Tables 1/7/11, and UEL's own Annual Report staff notes – to build the fullest view available, and marks clearly where a real gap remains (principally: FTE and cost are not available broken down by full-time/part-time or fixed-term/open-ended at all).

Academic staff (excluding atypical/guest staff), by mode and terms of employment

HESA Staff Table 7 ("HE academic staff (excluding atypicals) by HE provider, mode of employment... terms of employment..."), headcount, UEL-filtered (UKPRN 10007144). This explicitly excludes guest/HPL staff – see the panel below for that population. Cross-referenced in more depth in Reports/TUR01 (fixed-term share vs England sector average).

Guest / hourly-paid and specialist lecturers ("HPL"): headcount and cost

UEL's own Annual Report and Financial Statements, staff costs note, every year 2014/15–2024/25 (cross-validated via each report's prior-year comparative). This is the "academic atypical" population in HESA terms (see Reports/UDR07) – but the headcount and cost figures here come directly from UEL's own accounts, not from HESA. The salaries shown are already included within the total staff costs figure used in Reports/TUR02 and Reports/TUR13 – HPL pay is booked as a staff cost, not as "other operating expenditure".

FTE: what HESA actually publishes for UEL

HESA Staff Table 11 ("HE staff FTE by HE provider, cost centre, contract marker and academic year"), summed across all 14 UEL cost-centre groups, 2015/16–2024/25. This is the only genuine FTE (not headcount) figure available for UEL by staff category – but it is cut by cost centre, not by mode of employment or terms of employment, so it cannot be crossed with the full-time/part-time/fixed-term breakdown above.
CAVEAT The "Academic atypical" FTE line is small enough (0–20 FTE across the whole institution) that HESA's disclosure-control rounding (each cost-centre cell rounded independently to the nearest 5, many rounding to 0) makes the summed total unreliable as an exact figure – treat it as "genuinely small" rather than a precise number. 2024/25 sums to exactly 0 for this reason, not because atypical staff had zero FTE that year.

Two different ways UEL counts its own staff – and why they don't match HESA

Neither of these next two series comes from HESA. Both are UEL's own internal categorisations, published in the Annual Report, and neither is designed to reconcile with HESA's Staff Tables.
UEL's Annual Report separately publishes "Average staff numbers by major category" – a completely different four-way split (Senior Management / Teaching / Research / Professional, Administrative, Technical and Other Support) that explicitly excludes guest/HPL staff (stated directly in every report: "the number of staff paid cannot be readily converted into full time equivalents so has not been included in the staff number table above"). This is the categorisation behind UEL's headline "1,551 staff in 2024/25" figure, and it is not the same population as either HESA table above.

Reading these numbers

Part-time and fixed-term shares have both risen among standard-contract academic staff. Part-time staff rose from 46.7% of the academic (excl. atypical) headcount in 2014/15 to 52.3% in 2024/25 – now the majority. Fixed-term share rose from 31.9% to 42.7% over the same period, consistent with the pattern already documented in Reports/TUR01.
Guest/HPL cost has risen far faster than headcount, and roughly tracks the "other operating expenses" story in TUR13 in timing, if not in accounting treatment. Spend on hourly-paid and specialist lecturers fell to a low of £1.71m in 2018/19, then rose almost every year to a peak of £4.96m in 2023/24 (2.9×), before easing to £3.46m in 2024/25. Headcount over the same window moved far less consistently (559 → 711 → 641), meaning average cost per HPL member has grown substantially – consistent with more hours per person, higher hourly rates, or both. This spend sits inside the Staff Costs line, not Other Operating Expenses – a correction to a hypothesis raised when TUR13 was built.
HESA's own atypical-staff headcount and UEL's HPL headcount diverge, especially since 2022/23 (HESA: 455/535/430 for 2022/23–2024/25; UEL: 493/711/641) – before that they tracked reasonably closely (e.g. 2018/19: 550 vs 559). The most likely explanation is reference period: HESA's Staff Table 1 counts atypical staff active around a specific point in the year, while UEL's own note counts everyone paid at any point across the full August–July financial year – a whole-year cumulative count will run higher than a snapshot whenever there's turnover among casual staff, and the gap widening in the most recent two years may itself be a signal of increased HPL turnover, though that can't be confirmed from these figures alone.
CAVEATS HESA Table 7 figures (mode/terms of employment) exclude atypical staff by definition – they are not a full-institution count. HESA Table 11 FTE figures are summed across independently-rounded cost-centre cells, so small-category totals (especially "Academic atypical") should be read as approximate. UEL's own "average lecturer cost" FTE conversion for guest/HPL is explicitly flagged by UEL itself as an approximation, not a true FTE calculation. Figures are nominal (not inflation-adjusted) throughout.
