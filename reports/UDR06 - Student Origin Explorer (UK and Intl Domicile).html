Reading these numbers

Growth is almost entirely international. UK enrolment was essentially flat over ten years (14,060 in 2014/15 → 13,005 in 2024/25, dipping as low as 11,520 along the way), while Intl enrolment grew more than 7-fold (1,785 → 13,230). By 2024/25 Intl students are essentially half of UEL's entire population.
Within the UK side, Greater London alone supplies 61% of UEL's English intake (7,690 of 12,690 in 2024/25) – this is a fundamentally London-facing institution on the domestic side, and that hasn't changed.
LIMITATION Neither Table 28 nor Table 59 has a subject dimension, so nothing here can confirm whether Business and Computing specifically are where the international growth concentrated (only that postgraduate Intl numbers are much larger than undergraduate Intl numbers – see the Level of study filter above). Confirming a subject-level link would need HESA's raw Student record or Heidi Plus.
