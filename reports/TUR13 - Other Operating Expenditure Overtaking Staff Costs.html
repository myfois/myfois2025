TUR13 — Other Operating Expenditure Overtaking Staff Costs

UEL Annual Report and Financial Statements, Notes on staff costs and other operating expenses, 2014/15–2024/25. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

TRADE UNION REPORT SERIES · TUR13
HEADLINE "Other operating expenses" – everything that isn't pay, restructuring, depreciation or interest – overtook staff costs for the first time in 2022/23 (£121.5m vs £105.5m) and has stayed above it ever since, reaching £139.6m vs £124.3m in 2024/25. For most of the decade before that, other operating expenses ran at 60–75% of the staff cost total; they now sit at 112–117%.

What's driving the growth: two categories inside "other operating expenses"

Note 9's own activity breakdown, consolidated, £000s. These are the two lines the Annual Reports' own financial commentary repeatedly names as the reason for year-on-year increases in other operating expenditure.

Reading these numbers

The crossover is real and has now held for three consecutive years. Other operating expenses were consistently well below staff costs from 2014/15 to 2020/21 (60–79% of the staff cost figure). From 2021/22 the ratio climbed every year – 89% → 115% → 117% → 112% – overtaking staff costs in 2022/23 and never falling back below. As a share of total income, staff costs fell from a 2016/17 peak of 60.1% to 41.9% by 2024/25, while other operating expenses rose from a fairly flat 35–39% band to 47–49%.
"Administration and central services" is the single largest driver. This Note 9 sub-line grew from £13.1m (2017/18) to £61.1m (2024/25) – a 4.7× increase – including a 65% jump in one year alone (2021/22→2022/23, £30.3m→£49.8m). The Annual Reports' own financial commentary attributes this repeatedly, across three consecutive years, to "an increase in recruitment agent commission due to increased overseas recruitment" and, from 2022/23 onward, "the use of external consultancy."
"Academic departments" spend also jumped sharply from 2021/22 (£8.0m → £20.6m → £30.2m → £38.1m, before easing to £34.6m in 2024/25), a 5.7× increase from its 2017/18 base. The Annual Reports attribute this specifically to "the introduction of the UCFB partnership and payments made to partners" – i.e. this is money paid out to a franchise/validation partner, not spending on UEL's own academic departments despite the line's name.
This sits alongside two trends already documented elsewhere in this series: the international-income concentration in Reports/TUR11 (non-UK fees at 40.8% of total income by 2023/24, much of it plausibly recruited via the agent commission structure sitting inside "Administration and central services" here), and the renewed three-year climb in restructuring costs in Reports/TUR04 (£345k → £495k → £651k → £886k, 2021/22–2024/25). All three point to the same period, 2021/22 onward, as a structural turning point in UEL's cost base.
CAVEATS The Annual Reports do not itemise "Administration and central services" or "Academic departments" beyond these headline totals – the specific split between recruitment agent commission, external consultancy fees, UCFB partnership payments, and other items within each line is not disclosed in the published accounts and would need an FOI request or further internal disclosure to establish precisely.
