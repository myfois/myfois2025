UDR09 — UEL Enrolment Reconciliation

Comparing UEL's total enrolment as counted two independent ways – by subject of study, and by student domicile (UK/international) – across all 11 published years, 2014/15–2024/25. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

WHAT THIS IS UEL's enrolment can be counted in two independent ways that should, in principle, agree: by subject of study (every student counted once against their subject) and by domicile (every student counted once as UK-domiciled or international). This page checks whether they actually do agree, year by year and filter by filter – and flags it clearly where they don't.

Reading this

2014/15 through 2022/23: the two counts agree almost exactly. Across those nine years, the subject-based and domicile-based totals never differ by more than ±10 – exactly what you'd expect from two figures that are each independently rounded to the nearest 5.
FLAG 2023/24 and 2024/25 show a real, growing gap – not just rounding noise. At All levels/All modes, the subject-based count exceeds the domicile-based count by 60 in 2023/24 and by 520 in 2024/25. The most likely explanation is a small (and, in the newest year, larger) group of students whose domicile hadn't yet been fully classified at the time each table was published – they'd be counted once by subject but not yet assigned to either the UK or international domicile count. This isn't a sign the underlying data is wrong, but it does mean the 2024/25 international-growth figures elsewhere in this workspace are very slightly conservative (a small number of recent students aren't in either domicile bucket yet).
CAVEAT Figures are rounded to the nearest 5 by HESA's disclosure control; this reconciliation can only detect gaps larger than that rounding tolerance, not smaller ones.
