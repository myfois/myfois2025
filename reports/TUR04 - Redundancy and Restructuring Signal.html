TUR04 — Redundancy and Restructuring Signal

HESA Finance Table 13 (severance payments) and Staff Table 22 (academic staff starters and leavers), 2014/15–2024/25. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

TRADE UNION REPORT SERIES · TUR04
HEADLINE HESA's own severance data independently confirms every figure in this project's Annual-Report-derived restructuring trend, and adds something new: how many people were affected each year (156 in 2016/17, down to 10 in 2021/22, back up to 33 in 2024/25). Restructuring costs have climbed for three straight years (2021/22→2024/25: £345k→£886k), and academic staff turnover (starters + leavers) has grown sharply alongside the institution's overall growth in the last three years.

Severance costs and headcount affected

HESA Finance Table 13, "Aggregate of compensation for loss of office paid across the whole provider" (includes the head of provider). Independently confirms every figure already compiled from UEL's own Annual Reports in docs/VC_Pay_and_Financial_Trends_10yr.md, and additionally gives the number of people this was paid to each year – not previously available in one place in this project.

Academic staff starters and leavers

HESA Staff Table 22, all modes of employment. A spike in leavers, especially alongside a severance-cost spike the same or prior year, is a useful cross-check that a restructuring signal reflects real headcount movement rather than just accounting timing.

Reading these numbers

The core bargaining fact: restructuring/severance costs have risen for three consecutive years (2021/22–2024/25: £345k → £495k → £651k → £886k), and the number of people affected has grown alongside it (10 → 25 → 24 → 33). This is a genuine, independently-confirmed, cross-validated trend – not an artefact of one data source.
2016/17 (156 people, £3,969k) and 2019/20 (147 people, £3,604k) stand out as the two largest restructuring episodes in the decade by cost and headcount alike – both roughly 25× the cost of the lowest year (2018/19, £301k/20 people). 2020/21's negative figure (–£1,037k) is a genuine net credit: UEL's own narrative confirms the prior year's provision was overestimated and part of it was credited back.
Academic staff numbers grew substantially from 2021/22 onward (815 → 1,140 by 2023/24), driven by large starter intakes (270, 355, 330) that outpaced leavers in those years – consistent with the international-enrolment-driven growth story already documented in UDR05/UDR06. 2024/25 is the first year in this run where leavers (290) outnumbered starters (245), a net headcount fall of 45 – worth watching as a possible turning point.
CAVEATS Table 22's "Starters"/"Leavers" reflect all reasons for joining/leaving (retirement, resignation, end of fixed-term contract, redundancy) – it cannot isolate redundancy specifically as a reason. Table 13's severance figures cover all staff (not just those on the specific restructuring exercises named in the Annual Reports), so small routine severance payments are included alongside larger organised restructuring rounds. Figures rounded to the nearest 5 (Table 22) or exact £'000s (Table 13) per each table's own disclosure convention.
