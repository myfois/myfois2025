Reading these numbers

Nationally, research-only academics are by far the most likely to be on fixed-term contracts – 64.7% of England's research-only academic staff were fixed-term in 2024/25, compared with 33.1% of teaching-only staff and just 5.9% of staff who do both teaching and research. Research-only contracts being tied to specific, time-limited grant funding is the well-known structural reason for this – a pattern documented across the sector for years and not specific to UEL, but not something UEL is likely to be exempt from either.
At UEL, research-only academic staff have been a small and fairly stable group throughout the decade – between 25 and 50 people (headcount), or roughly 3–5.5% of total academic staff, with a gentle downward drift in share (5.5% in 2014/15 to around 3–4% in most recent years) even as total academic staff numbers have grown substantially. This is consistent with UEL's own institutional profile as a teaching-intensive, rather than research-intensive, university – but it also means any precarity affecting this small group is easy for aggregate staff statistics to obscure.
CAVEATS "Research only" in HESA's classification means staff whose contracted duties are entirely research – it excludes staff on "Both teaching and research" contracts who may still hold fixed-term research-funded posts alongside teaching duties, so the true number of precariously-employed research-active staff at UEL is very likely higher than the "research only" headcount alone suggests. This report cannot quantify that broader group from HESA's open data. A genuine UEL-specific answer to "how many of our research-only (or research-active) staff are on fixed-term contracts" would need UEL's own HR data, not HESA's aggregated tables.
