Reading these numbers

Asia is effectively the entire story: Asia grew from 520 non-UK students in 2014/15 to 12,090 in 2024/25 – from 29% to 91% of all non-UK enrolment. India alone accounts for 7,965 of UEL's 13,230 non-UK students in 2024/25 (60%), with Nepal (2,410) and Pakistan (1,210) the next largest and both growing fastest in just the last 1–2 years.
OFFSETTING TREND The European Union total fell every year on record: 565 in 2014/15 down to 130 in 2024/25 – consistent with the post-Brexit change in EU fee status and loan eligibility. Non-EU/Asian growth has more than made up for this, but it means "international" at UEL today looks structurally different from ten years ago, not just larger.
Growth has been more postgraduate than undergraduate: postgraduate non-UK enrolment reached 8,990 in 2024/25 versus 4,240 undergraduate – roughly two-thirds postgraduate. That's relevant context for the Business/Computing enrolment story elsewhere in this analysis, since it points toward taught master's recruitment as a major driver, though Table 28 alone can't confirm which subjects those postgraduates are in.

This still can't be joined to subject (Business, Computing, etc.) or to NSS directly – Table 28 has no subject dimension. It answers "has UEL's student body become more international, and from where" on its own terms; it does not by itself prove that Business/Computing's NSS trends are being driven by this shift.
