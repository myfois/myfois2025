What's actually in this data

Methodology, straight from the source file
LEVEL OF STUDY The download's own filter line reads: Level of study: All. That means every HESA level is summed together in every number on this page – First degree (bachelor's), Other undergraduate (foundation degrees, HNDs/HNCs, CertHE/DipHE – roughly Levels 4–5), Postgraduate taught (MAs, MScs, PGCE) and Postgraduate research (PhDs, research masters) are all combined into one figure per subject per year. So yes, this includes MAs and other postgraduate students, not just undergraduates.
NO LEVEL 3 HESA only covers Higher Education – it doesn't collect data on Level 3 (A-level/BTEC-equivalent, further education) students at all, regardless of the level filter. So there's no Level 3 contamination here; the "floor" of what's included is Other undergraduate (roughly Level 4–5), not anything pre-HE.
HOW TO DIFFERENTIATE To split this by level, you'd re-download Table 49 once per level – setting the "Level of study" filter to First degree, Other undergraduate, Postgraduate (taught), or Postgraduate (research) individually instead of "All" – and get four separate provider×subject grids. HESA's Table 2 ("HE student enrolment FTE by HE provider and level of study") gives the level breakdown directly, but without the subject dimension, so it's a good cross-check on totals but won't tell you e.g. how much of Computing's growth is postgraduate vs undergraduate on its own.
DOMICILE No – confirmed again on this specific extract. The filter list has Country/Region of HE provider (i.e. where UEL itself is based), Level of study, Mode of study, and Academic year. There is no domicile, nationality, or fee-status filter or column anywhere in Table 49. That dimension only exists in Table 52 (subject × domicile, national totals, no provider split) or in HESA's raw record data / Heidi Plus.
SCOPE This covers every enrolled student, every level, every mode, in a given academic year – undergraduate and postgraduate, first-year through final-year, full-time and part-time. It is not the same population as NSS (which surveys final-year undergraduates only) and the two should not be read as directly comparable head counts. This page is about enrolment patterns on their own terms.

Reading these numbers

Business and management is UEL's largest subject by a wide margin: 9,075 enrolments in 2024/25, up 71% from 5,305 in 2021/22. Computing more than doubled over the same period (1,965 → 4,645, +136%) and is now the second-largest subject. Together they went from 37% of all UEL enrolments in 2021/22 to 51% in 2024/25.
Three subjects shrank noticeably over the same four years: Social sciences (−34%), Psychology and Education and teaching (both −25%). Total UEL enrolment still grew 37% overall, so this is concentration – growth in a few large subjects outpacing, and partly offsetting, decline elsewhere – rather than uniform expansion.

This page deliberately doesn't cross-reference NSS scores – the two datasets cover different student populations (all-levels enrolment vs. final-year-undergraduate survey respondents) and mixing them risks implying a comparison the data doesn't actually support. If you want to explore enrolment growth alongside NSS trends specifically, treat that as a separate, clearly-labelled analysis rather than a merged table.
