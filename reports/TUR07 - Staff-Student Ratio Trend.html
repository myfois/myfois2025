2022/23 chosen deliberately as the last year before the Table 37 data-quality anomaly. Cost centres with zero or negligible staff/student FTE (Agriculture, Humanities – both effectively inactive at UEL) are excluded.

Reading these numbers

The institution-wide ratio roughly doubled between 2015/16 and 2022/23 (16.4 → 24.9 students per academic FTE), a genuine and substantial trend that pre-dates the 2023/24–2024/25 data anomaly and is not explained by it. Academic FTE grew only modestly over this period (685 → 670), while student FTE grew by roughly half (11,245 → 16,670).
Subject-level ratios in 2022/23 vary enormously – from 14.4 (Biological, mathematical & physical sciences) to 56.8 (Administration & business studies). This spread partly reflects genuine differences in teaching model (e.g. large lecture-based business programmes vs smaller lab/practical-based science programmes) and partly reflects how HESA's cost-centre coding attributes shared/service teaching – it should prompt a conversation with local reps in the highest-ratio areas rather than be read as a precise workload measure on its own.
CAVEATS This is an institution/subject-level FTE ratio, not a class-size or contact-hours measure – it says nothing about actual teaching group sizes, non-teaching duties, or research time. Table 11's cost-centre categories are staff-contract-based; Table 37's are module/registration-based, so the two aren't perfectly aligned at the subject level even before the 2023/24 anomaly. The 2023/24 and 2024/25 ratios (18.7 and 30.2) should not be quoted without the caveat above.
