TUR02 — Staff Pay Bill vs. Staff and Student Numbers

HESA Finance Table 12 (staff costs), Finance Table 1 (total income), Student Table 37 (student FTE), ONS CPIH (inflation), 2016/17–2024/25. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

TRADE UNION REPORT SERIES · TUR02
HEADLINE Staff costs have not kept pace with UEL's total income: the staff pay bill has fallen from 60.2% of total income in 2016/17 to 41.9% in 2024/25 — even though staff costs have grown in real (inflation-adjusted) terms too, income has grown much faster. The "no money for a pay rise" claim doesn't hold up against the institution's own overall resources — the pay bill's share of a much bigger pie has shrunk by nearly a fifth.

Staff costs as a share of total income

The core test: is spending on staff keeping pace with the institution's overall resources? Both figures are HESA-reported consolidated totals, so this ratio isn't affected by inflation (both numerator and denominator are same-year cash terms).

Staff costs and total income, nominal vs. real terms

Real-terms figures are deflated to 2024/25 prices using ONS CPIH (annual rate compounded year-on-year, financial year approximated to the calendar year it ends in – see caveats). Even adjusting for inflation, staff costs have risen – the issue is that income has risen faster.

DATA CAVEAT The student FTE figure (HESA Student Table 37, "Total academic cost centres") shows an unexplained dip in 2023/24 (12,915, down from 16,670 the year before) before jumping to 20,955 in 2024/25 – this doesn't match the steady enrolment growth shown elsewhere in this project (UDR04, UDR09, the annual reports' own headcount of 42,062 students in 2024/25). Cost-centre mapping is a known HESA area where definitions and provider submissions can shift year to year (already flagged in this project's catalogue documentation) – treat the 2023/24 figure specifically as an artefact of that, not a real one-year drop in student numbers, and treat "income per student FTE" in that year with caution.

Reading these numbers

The core bargaining fact: staff costs as a share of total income fell every single year in this series bar one small tick, from 60.2% (2016/17) to 41.9% (2024/25) – a fall of 18.3 percentage points. Meanwhile staff costs in real terms (2024/25 prices) actually grew modestly (£106.2m → £124.3m), so this isn't a story of the pay bill being cut – it's a story of the institution's income growing much faster than what it spends on staff, largely on the back of international student fee growth (see TUR11 and UDR06 for the enrolment side of that story).
Staff cost per staff FTE rose from £60.7k (2016/17) to £80.2k (2024/25) in nominal terms – but this is an average across a headcount that includes very different roles and grades, so it says nothing about whether typical individual pay has kept pace with inflation. For that, see the separate NSS/casualisation reports (TUR01) and the sector-wide pay-award history published by UCU nationally, which sits outside this project's data.
CAVEATS The CPIH deflator approximates each financial year (Aug–Jul) to the calendar year it ends in (e.g. 2016/17 ≈ calendar 2017) – a simplification, not an exact match to UEL's financial year. "Total staff numbers (FTE) as disclosed in accounts" (Finance Table 12) is UEL's own reported figure in its statutory accounts, not independently verified by HESA. Student FTE figures (Table 37) have a known volatility issue in the most recent two years – see the flag above.
