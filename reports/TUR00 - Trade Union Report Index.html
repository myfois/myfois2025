TUR00 — Trade Union Report Index

Index of all Trade Union Reports (TUR01–TUR12) in this folder. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

Click any report title below to open it. Each report is a self-contained HTML file with its exact data sources listed in its own footer. These 12 reports were built from the proposal in docs/Potential_Future_Reports_Trade_Union_Briefings_v2.md, using only data that is genuinely available and UEL-specific (or clearly flagged where it isn't) – see that document for the fuller rationale behind each report and what was originally envisioned vs what HESA/OfS/NSS open data can actually support.
A few reports are explicit about the limits of what open data can show. TUR05 and TUR09 flag that HESA's ethnicity and disability tables are England-wide only, with no UEL breakdown possible. TUR08 reports an honest null result (NSS scores rose every year despite a rising staff-student ratio) rather than forcing a conclusion the data doesn't support. TUR07 and TUR02 both carry the same data-quality caveat about an anomaly in Student Table 37's 2023/24–2024/25 figures.
Pay and workforce cost
01

Casualisation Explorer

Fixed-term vs permanent academic contracts at UEL, 2014/15–2024/25, benchmarked against England-wide sector data.

Staff Pay Bill vs Staff and Student Numbers

Staff costs as a share of income, nominal vs real-terms (CPIH-deflated) trends, and spending per head, 2016/17–2024/25.

03

Senior Pay vs Staff Pay Differential

Vice-Chancellor pay and VC-to-median-staff pay ratio, cross-validating HESA Finance Table 11 against UEL's own Annual Reports, 2016/17–2024/25.

Redundancy, restructuring and financial risk
04

Redundancy and Restructuring Signal

Severance costs, headcount affected, and academic staff turnover, 2014/15–2024/25.

10

Financial Health and Risk Explorer

HESA's own Key Financial Indicators – surplus/deficit, liquidity, reserves, borrowing – 2015/16–2024/25, with OfS sector context.

11

International Reliance and Redundancy Risk

Growth of international tuition fee income as a share of total institutional income, 2016/17–2023/24 – the revenue concentration behind restructuring risk.

Equality and staffing profile
05

Staff Equality Profile

Academic staff by nationality (UEL-specific) and gender pay gap trend, 2014/15–2024/25 – with an explicit note on what HESA's ethnicity/disability tables cannot show at UEL level.

06

Student Equality vs Staffing

UEL-specific undergraduate entrant diversity (OfS Access and Participation data) set against total staff headcount growth, 2018/19–2024/25.

09

Research Staff Precarity

UEL's research-only academic staff trend, set against a striking England-wide finding: research-only staff are far more likely to be on fixed-term contracts than any other academic function group.

Workload
07

Staff-Student Ratio Trend

Students per academic FTE, institution-wide and by subject, 2015/16–2024/25 – the ratio has roughly doubled since 2015/16.

Data-quality caveat on 2023/24–2024/25
08

NSS as Workload Barometer

Tests whether NSS satisfaction on staff-facing themes tracks the rising staff-student ratio – reports an honest null result, with the reasons why this isn't a clean test.
