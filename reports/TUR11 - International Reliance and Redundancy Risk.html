TRADE UNION REPORT SERIES · TUR11
HEADLINE International (non-UK domiciled) tuition fee income has grown from 14.5% of UEL's total institutional income in 2016/17 to 40.8% by 2023/24 – nearly tripling its share in seven years. This is the flip side of the growth story already documented in student numbers (UDR05/UDR06) and staff numbers (TUR02): the institution's financial health, staffing levels and job security are now substantially more exposed to anything that reduces international recruitment (visa policy changes, currency shifts, competitor country policy, geopolitical events) than they were a decade ago.

Reading these numbers

The growth is real and substantial, not a rounding effect. Non-UK fee income rose from £19.2m (2016/17) to £114.1m (2023/24) – a six-fold increase in cash terms, while UK fee income grew much more modestly (£78.2m → £107.9m, up about 38%). Total institutional income nearly doubled over the same period (£132.6m → £279.5m), and non-UK fees supplied the large majority of that additional growth.
This connects directly to the redundancy and restructuring pattern already documented in Reports/TUR04: restructuring costs and headcount reductions have risen in three of the last four years for which data is available, at the same time as the institution has become steadily more dependent on a single, externally volatile revenue stream. A shock to international recruitment (a not-infrequent occurrence across the UK sector in recent years) would remove a much larger share of total income today than it would have in 2016/17.
CAVEATS This report shows financial exposure, not a prediction – it does not show that a downturn is coming, only that the institution's income structure is now more concentrated in one variable revenue source than a decade ago. The EU/Non-EU merge into "Non-UK" from 2022/23 means the pre/post-Brexit domicile mix within that combined figure can't be tracked separately in the most recent two years from this table alone. Figures are nominal (not inflation-adjusted); see Reports/TUR02 for the CPIH-deflated real-terms treatment of UEL's total income over a similar period.
