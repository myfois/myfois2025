SCOPE LIMIT HESA's open data only lets us build a genuine UEL-specific equality picture for nationality (Staff Table 24). The equivalent tables for ethnicity (Table 4) and disability (Table 5) are published by HESA only at England/sector level – they contain no institution field, so no UEL breakdown can be extracted from them at all. Those two are shown below as national context only, not as UEL figures. Gender is covered separately via UEL's own statutory Gender Pay Gap disclosures, which are UEL-specific.

What HESA's open data cannot show at UEL level: ethnicity and disability

Table 4 (ethnicity) and Table 5 (disability) are England/sector-wide only – shown here as national benchmarks for context, not as UEL figures. Both are 2024/25, academic staff, England.
England-wide, academic staff of known ethnicity in 2024/25 were 26.4% Black, Asian or another minority ethnic background (73.6% White). 8.5% of academic staff with known disability status reported a disability. These are sector-wide figures – without UEL's own HR/EDI monitoring data, there is no way to know whether UEL's own staff profile on these two dimensions sits above, below, or in line with this national picture.

Reading these numbers

Nationality: the non-UK share of UEL's academic staff has been on a U-shaped path – 37.4% (2014/15) down to a low of 28.5% (2021/22), then back up to 35.2% (2024/25), driven almost entirely by growth in non-EU staff (110 in 2019/20 → 220 in 2024/25, roughly doubling) while EU staff numbers have stayed broadly flat (115–145 throughout) – consistent with the post-Brexit shift from EU to international recruitment already seen in UEL's student numbers (UDR05/UDR06).
Gender pay gap: the median hourly gap has narrowed from 9.3% (2018) to a low of 4.7% (2023), but has since ticked back up slightly to 5.1% (2025) – it has not closed, and has been essentially flat in the 4.7–5.1% range for three consecutive snapshots. Women's share of the highest-paid quarter has risen from 44.0% to 50.0% over the same period, reaching parity with the lower-paid quarters only in the last two years.
CAVEATS Table 24's nationality categories (UK/EU/non-EU/not known) are not the same as ethnicity, and "not known" can be a non-trivial share in some years (up to 65 staff, 2014/15). The Gender Pay Gap disclosure is an organisation-wide snapshot at one date per year – it cannot be broken down by grade, contract type, faculty, or intersected with nationality or ethnicity, so it cannot on its own support a full equal-pay audit. The 31 March 2021 bonus-gap figures are excluded from the chart below as statistically meaningless (only 0.2%/0.1% of staff received any bonus that year); see the summary markdown for detail.
