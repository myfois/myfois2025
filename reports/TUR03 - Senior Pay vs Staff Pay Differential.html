HEADLINE Two independent sources agree: HESA's own open data (Finance Table 11) and UEL's Annual Report disclosures give near-identical VC pay-multiple figures for every year they overlap (2019/20–2022/23). The pay multiple has run in a 5.6–6.9 range across the last six years, peaking in 2022/23, with basic salary rising every year since 2018/19 (£201k–225k → £265.3k in 2024/25).

2017/18 in detail — three post-holders, three part-year figures

UEL changed Vice-Chancellor mid-year in both 2017/18 and 2018/19. HESA's Finance Table 11 captures each post-holder's own part-year figures separately – there is no single "2017/18 VC pay ratio" because there wasn't one continuous post-holder that year.

Reading these numbers

The core bargaining fact: since Prof. Amanda Broderick became sole Vice-Chancellor (from September 2018), the pay multiple has moved in a narrow, fairly stable 5.6–6.9 range, basic-to-median and total-to-median respectively, with a peak in 2022/23 (6.1/6.9). Basic salary has risen every year since 2018/19 without exception, from roughly £201–225k to £265.3k in 2024/25 (per the Annual Report; HESA's Table 11 only currently extends to 2022/23).
The close agreement between HESA's independently-collected Table 11 figures and UEL's own Annual Report figures (both landing on 5.6/6.9 for 2019/20, 5.6/6.4 for 2020/21, 5.6/6.1 for 2021/22, and 6.1/6.9 for 2022/23) is itself useful: it means this isn't a case of one source's numbers being disputable against another – two independently-compiled records agree.
CAVEATS HESA's Finance Table 11 currently only extends to 2022/23 in the data held for this project – 2023/24 and 2024/25 figures here come from the Annual Report only (already fully verified, see docs/VC_Pay_and_Financial_Trends_10yr.md). The 2018/19 "basic salary paid" figure from HESA (£201k) is noticeably lower than the Annual Report's figure for the same post-holder (£225k) – this is very likely because Prof. Broderick started 10 September 2018, so HESA's "paid" figure reflects roughly 10.5 months' actual pay in that financial year rather than an annualised salary; not treated as a discrepancy requiring further investigation, but worth knowing if quoting the 2018/19 figure specifically. Figures are in £'000s and rounded per each source's own convention.
