TUR12 — UEL vs Sector Benchmarking

OfS Condition B3 student outcomes, NSS 2026, and KEF Cluster J knowledge exchange data. University of East London (UKPRN 10007144).

TRADE UNION REPORT SERIES · TUR12
MIXED PICTURE Bringing together three independent sector-benchmarking sources gives a genuinely mixed picture, not a uniform "above" or "below" story: UEL is at or above its statistical benchmark on continuation, completion and every NSS satisfaction theme, but below benchmark on graduate progression and below its comparator cluster average on most knowledge-exchange/business-engagement metrics.

OfS student outcomes: UEL vs statistical benchmark

OfS Condition B3 indicators, full-time First Degree, "taught or registered" population. Continuation and Progression cover the 2019-2022 cohort; Completion covers the 2016-2019 cohort (OfS's own most recent published aggregation for each measure).

NSS 2026: UEL vs benchmark, all seven themes

National Student Survey, all undergraduates, all subjects, most recent year (2026). Benchmark is OfS/HESA's own calculated figure for a provider with UEL's subject/mode/level mix. See Reports/TUR08 for the full multi-year trend.

Knowledge Exchange Framework: UEL's engagement level vs its comparator cluster

KEF5, UEL is grouped in Cluster J (a peer group of institutions with a similar subject mix, research intensity and size, defined by KEF's own clustering methodology). Each metric is scored on a 5-level engagement scale relative to the whole English sector, not just the cluster.

Reading these numbers

UEL performs at or above its statistical benchmark on the measures closest to day-to-day teaching quality: completion (85.5% vs 80.6% benchmark, +4.9pp), continuation (85.5% vs 84.4%, +1.1pp), and every one of the seven NSS themes in 2026 (Teaching on my course +3.4pp, Assessment and feedback +5.7pp, Academic support +0.2pp, and so on). This is a genuinely strong, benchmark-beating result on the measures most directly shaped by day-to-day staff effort.
Progression (graduate outcomes 15 months after completion) sits below benchmark (65.9% vs 67.6%, –1.7pp) – this measure is more exposed to factors outside teaching staff's direct control (local labour market conditions, graduate employer demand, student demographics) than continuation or completion are, but it is still a genuine gap worth tracking alongside the London/graduate labour market context.
KEF: below cluster on business/knowledge-exchange engagement UEL scores "Low" or "Very low" engagement on 8 of its 16 KEF metrics (mostly IP/commercialisation and working with non-SME business/public sector), against 6 "High"/"Very high" scores concentrated in CPD, graduate start-ups, public engagement, and collaborative research funding. This reflects UEL's profile as a teaching-intensive, locally-engaged institution rather than a commercialisation-focused one – not necessarily a problem, but relevant context if institutional strategy documents cite "knowledge exchange income growth" as a rationale for restructuring, since that income stream is a comparatively small and uneven part of UEL's current profile.
CAVEATS OfS B3 benchmarks are statistical adjustments for each provider's own student population mix (age, entry qualifications, subject, deprivation, etc.) – "beating benchmark" means outperforming what would be expected given who UEL recruits, not outperforming other providers in absolute terms. NSS benchmarks work the same way. KEF engagement levels are sector-wide quintiles, not cluster-specific rankings, so "Low engagement" does not necessarily mean UEL trails its Cluster J peers specifically – only the whole English sector.
