Does this show up in retention data? — UEL vs. sector, HESA Tables T3d & T5

A different HESA collection (UK Performance Indicators: non-continuation) tracks what happens to entrants, rather than what award qualifiers eventually receive. These two tables test whether "Other undergraduate" qualifiers are linked to weaker retention.
Table T3d — non-continuation of "Other undergraduate" entrants (UK, full-time, UEL vs. sector benchmark). Tracks whether entrants are still in HE the following year. UEL's "no longer in HE" rate for this group has been consistently at or above the sector benchmark, and reached 25.2% in the 2019/20 entry cohort – the last year this series covers.
Table T5 — projected outcomes for First-degree starters (2019/20 entry cohort, the only year in this file). Of UEL's 3,265 UK full-time first-degree starters, HESA projects 9.4% will end up qualifying with a lower ("Other") award instead of the degree they started – almost double the sector benchmark of 4.8%. This is the closest direct evidence in the available data that UEL's elevated "Other undergraduate" qualifier numbers reflect first-degree entrants falling short of the honours degree, rather than students who chose a sub-degree course from the outset.
CAVEAT T3d and T5 are a genuinely different HESA collection from Table 16 (UK Performance Indicators: non-continuation, not the qualifiers table), covering UK-domiciled full-time entrants only, and they were discontinued after the 2020/21 edition – 2019/20 is the final entry-cohort year HESA published under this methodology. There is no more recent official version to update this with; successor measures (continuation/completion rates) are now published by the Office for Students under Condition B3 and the TEF, not by HESA. T3d and Table 16 also can't be reconciled numerically – T3d counts entrants in a cohort year, Table 16 counts qualifiers in an award year, so a rate in one table is not a share of a count in the other.

The current picture — OfS Student Outcomes Data Dashboard (Condition B3)

HESA's T3/T5 series stopped at the 2019/20 entry cohort; the Office for Students now publishes the successor continuation/completion measures used to regulate condition B3. Unlike T3/T5, the OfS data is a live series – but it only publishes one current 3-year rolling window per indicator, not a year-by-year history, so this is a snapshot comparison rather than a trend.
Other Undergraduate's completion rate is close to First Degree's – it's continuation where the gap shows up. UEL's Other Undergraduate continuation rate (79.6%, entrants 2019/20–2021/22) sits just below its own sector benchmark (80.7%) and clearly below UEL's First Degree continuation rate (84.8%). Completion is closer between the two levels (72.3% vs 85.2%) but Other Undergraduate's much smaller cohort (denominator 160 vs 13,360) makes that figure less precise.
CAVEAT This is a different population definition again – OfS "Other Undergraduate" (Taught population, full-time) is a course-classification concept, not the same as HESA Table 16's "Other undergraduate" qualifiers or Table T3d's entrants; the three are related but not numerically identical. OfS windows are also non-overlapping and don't line up year-for-year with the HESA T3d series above (continuation: entrants 2019/20–2021/22; completion: entrants 2016/17–2018/19). Figures shown use the "Taught" population, Full-time mode, "Overall" (no demographic split).

Does this break down by subject? — Other Undergraduate continuation & completion by CAH2 subject

The OfS CSV carries a subject-of-study split (CAH2 level). Most cells are too small to publish (HESA/OfS suppress cohorts below their disclosure threshold), but the ones that do survive line up with the named courses in the Awards Catalogue – "Subjects allied to medicine" is almost certainly the Nursing Associate FdSc population.

Reading these numbers

What is "Other undergraduate," specifically? Per HESA's own published definition, it's a catch-all for sub-bachelor's undergraduate qualifications that a provider doesn't report as a separate named category – things like Certificate of Higher Education (CertHE), Diploma of Higher Education (DipHE), other undergraduate diplomas and certificates, professional qualifications at undergraduate level, and post-registration health/social care awards. Foundation degree and HNC/HND are excluded from it here because UEL reports those two separately elsewhere in this same table. At UEL it's substantial – 1,390 qualifiers in 2024/25, more than Foundation degree – but HESA's open data doesn't break this bucket down further by course type, so which specific awards make it up (and in what proportions) isn't answerable from this table alone; it would need UEL's own course catalogue or HESA's restricted-access record-level data (Heidi Plus) to pin down further.
The First-class share has more than doubled. First class honours went from around 17–18% of UEL's first-degree qualifiers in 2014/15 to around 44% in 2024/25 (exact denominator varies slightly depending on whether it's measured against the "First degree" qualifier count or the sum of classifications, due to HESA's independent rounding of each), while Upper second (2:1) stayed roughly flat and Lower second/Third class both fell sharply – the same "grade inflation" pattern documented across the UK sector over this period, not something specific to UEL.
Postgraduate-taught qualifiers grew roughly 7.5x. Master's taught awards rose from 850 in 2014/15 to 6,385 in 2024/25 – consistent with the postgraduate-taught international enrolment growth documented in the Student Origin Explorer, though this table can't confirm the link directly (no domicile dimension here).
CAVEAT Master's, PGCE, and other non-first-degree awards genuinely are graded in practice (typically Distinction/Merit/Pass, or Pass/Fail) – but HESA's published qualifiers table only breaks classification out for First degree qualifiers. The Distinction/Merit/Pass split for other awards isn't in this open dataset, so it can't be shown here; that's a publication gap, not a claim that those awards are ungraded.
CAVEAT This table has no domicile or subject dimension, so classification trends can't be split by UK/Intl or by department. Figures are rounded to the nearest 5 by HESA's disclosure control; category sums may not exactly match published totals.
