These are different populations measured differently, but the near-doubling gap between UEL's student disability rate (~15%) and the national staff disability rate is a useful prompt for a conversation about support and workload for staff working with an increasingly diverse disabled student population.

Reading these numbers

The entrant population's ethnic composition has shifted year to year, not moved in one steady direction. White entrants' share of the full-time UK undergraduate intake rose from 32.8% (2018/19) to a peak of 46.3% (2022/23) before easing back to 39.1% (2023/24), while Black entrants' share fell from 35.3% (2018/19) to 27–30% across the most recent three years. Total entrant numbers dipped to a pandemic-affected low in 2021/22 (2,830) before recovering to 3,380 by 2023/24, above the 2018/19 starting point.
Total staff headcount has grown considerably faster than the entrant population, especially since 2021/22. The staff index rose steadily across the whole period (100 → 141.8 by 2024/25), while the entrant index dipped to a low of 86.8 in 2021/22 before only partially recovering to 103.7 by 2023/24. The gap between the two lines has widened sharply since 2021/22: staff headcount grew roughly 26% from 2021/22 to 2024/25 while entrants grew about 19% from their 2021/22 low, off a smaller base. Whether this staff growth has actually kept pace with real workload (as opposed to headcount alone, and across non-entrant students too) is exactly the question TUR07 (staff-student ratio) and TUR08 (NSS as workload barometer) are built to test.
CAVEATS OfS AP figures are rounded to the nearest 10 and describe UK-domiciled full-time undergraduate entrants only – they exclude postgraduates, part-time students, and international students, so they are not a full picture of UEL's student body (compare with the fuller enrolment series in UDR05/UDR06/TUR02). The ethnicity categories used by OfS (White/Black/Asian/Mixed/Other) are broader groupings than HESA's own more granular ethnicity categories used in Table 4. Staff Table 6 counts headcount, not FTE, and covers all staff (academic and non-academic) – it is not restricted to teaching-facing roles.
