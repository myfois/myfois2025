HEADLINE Table 14 is HESA's own official set of financial health ratios – the same kind of indicators OfS uses for sector-wide monitoring – and it independently confirms the 2019/20 crisis year already documented from UEL's Annual Reports (surplus/deficit of –44.5% of income that year, matching the £69m deficit in docs/VC_Pay_and_Financial_Trends_10yr.md). It also shows a genuine, sustained recovery since: external borrowing has been fully paid off since 2020/21, and by 2024/25 UEL's net liquidity (113 days) sits close to the sector "medium provider" average (111 days) reported by OfS for the same year.

Reading these numbers

2019/20 was a genuine, sharp crisis year, not a data artefact: surplus/deficit swung from +1.2% (2018/19) to –44.5% (2019/20) – entirely consistent with the one-off £69m building impairment already identified from UEL's own Annual Report. Every other year in the decade shows a surplus (or in 2015/16–2016/17, small deficits of –4.5% and –8.1% consistent with pre-2019/20 pressures), with a clear recovery to consistent, healthy surpluses (3–10%) from 2020/21 onward.
External borrowing has been eliminated: from 19.0% of income in 2015/16 down to essentially zero from 2020/21 onward – a genuinely de-risked balance sheet on this specific measure, removing one channel of financial pressure that might otherwise translate into cost-cutting.
Staff costs as a share of income have fallen steadily and substantially (57.2% in 2015/16 to 41.9% in 2024/25) – the same trend already identified in Reports/TUR02 from a different data source (Finance Table 12), now independently confirmed here. This fall reflects income growing faster than the pay bill, not the pay bill shrinking in absolute terms (see TUR02 for the full picture).
CAVEATS The 2019/20 "Unrestricted reserves as a % of total income" figure (–7.5%) reflects the same actuarial pension revaluation and impairment effects documented in the Annual Report analysis, not a cash reserves crisis in the everyday sense. Sector comparison figures are aggregate/typology averages from the OfS report, not a UEL-specific benchmark – OfS does not identify individual providers in this report (confirmed by a full-text search for "East London": zero matches). Current ratio has been below 1.0 in the two most recent years (0.9), which can be a genuine short-term liquidity watch-point even alongside healthy net liquidity days – the two measures capture different things and shouldn't be read as contradictory.
